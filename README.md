# About Me

I'm a life-long runner, learner, and builder. Hopefully I'll share something here that helps you to enjoy one of these things, too.

## Running

* EDM backed by Nike's Coach Bennett, with a cool tailwind off the Hudson River waterfront is my favorite physical metaphor
* My AirPods died at Mile 11 of the 2024 NYC Marathon, the energy of the crowd carried me, and I learned how important it is to cheer on others
* [Runsetta](https://github.com/collinbentley1/runsetta) is a project I'm incubating to share the profound motivation and connectedness I feel through this sport with others 
* Up Next > NYRR Midnight Run > NYE 2026

## Learning

* I don't remember most of what I've learned, especially facts, and this bothers me on occasion, but I'm also reminded that skills reflect my learning
* Debugging C code for years and having experiences like building a toy Turing machine with scraps of paper has taught both patience and respect for code
* Getting my PADI Advanced SCUBA license doesn't make me any more capable of reading a compass underwater, but now I know how to relax and catch my breath under stress
* Absorbing other languages hard for me; while my Chinese fades alongside ASL and French, coming back to the table is noticeably easier each time
* Teaching my students in Beijing continues to fill me with hope that all learning is possible for every person, and there's joy to find in being part of others' journeys to the same place

## Building

* Making with AGI feels better to me when it's like sitting down for a bit to continue chiseling on marble, rather than never leaving my seat so I can be ready to pull the slot machine's lever
* Making something new is exciting, so I find it especially sweet when other people like it
* On the other hand, bitter is trying to reverse engineer for others wanting or needing my thing
* Conviction can't be manufactured, and acknowledging when I'm out of energy is crucial
* I rest easy knowing that nothing has to totally work today since most people will try the thing that truly works tomorrow
